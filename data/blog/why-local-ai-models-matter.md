# Why Local AI Models Matter for Bangladesh and South Asia

For the past two years, the global AI conversation has been dominated by massive models trained in Silicon Valley, Beijing, and London. These models are powerful — but they weren't built for Bangladesh. They don't understand Bengali idioms. They don't know about bKash, Nagad, or local payment systems. They can't help you write a Facebook ad that resonates with a Bangladeshi audience.

**Local AI models matter because they understand local context.**

## The Problem with One-Size-Fits-All AI

### Cultural Blind Spots

When you ask a global AI model to write a marketing video script for "Eid shopping," it might generate content about Christmas shopping instead. These models lack the cultural grounding to produce relevant content for Bangladeshi audiences. Bengali holidays, traditions, social norms, and humor are simply outside their training data.

### Language Barriers

While models have improved with Bengali, they still struggle with:

- **Code-switching** — Most Bangladeshis naturally mix Bengali and English (Banglish). Global models handle this poorly.
- **Regional dialects** — The Bengali spoken in Sylhet differs from Barisal or Chattogram.
- **Contextual translation** — Direct translation from English produces stilted, unnatural Bengali.
- **Bangla script rendering** — Many models mangle Unicode Bengali text.

### Cost and Latency

Every API call to a foreign AI provider means data leaving Bangladesh, paying in foreign currency, and dealing with network latency. For real-time applications like video generation or AI chat, this creates a poor user experience.
